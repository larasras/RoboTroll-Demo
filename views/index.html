Heather Cockle looked at the stripy torch in her hands and felt concerned. She walked over to the window and reflected on her noisy surroundings. She had always loved snooty San Diego with its sweaty, sour swamps. It was a place that encouraged her tendency to feel concerned. Then she saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Reginald Barker. Reginald was a selfish giant with hairy elbows and red lips.
